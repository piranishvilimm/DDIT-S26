# Yoo, Lyytinen, and Boland: Digital Innovation Architecture

## Why this reading matters

This course is not only about cases and business headlines. It is also about understanding what makes digital innovation structurally different from earlier forms of innovation.

Yoo, Lyytinen, and Boland explain why digital systems behave differently.

## Key concepts

### Layered architecture
Digital systems are built in layers such as devices, networks, services, applications, and data. Change in one layer can create consequences in others.

### Reprogrammability
Digital components can be modified without rebuilding the entire physical system.

### Generativity
Digital infrastructures enable third parties to build, adapt, recombine, and extend functionality.

### Distributed innovation
Innovation no longer sits only inside one firm's R&D boundary. It emerges across ecosystems.

## Strategic implications

- Industry boundaries become more fluid.
- Entry barriers shift.
- Platform control matters.
- Complementors matter.
- Innovation speed increases.
- Coordination becomes harder but more powerful.
