# Participation Rubric

## Why participation matters

This course runs as a seminar, not as a passive lecture block. With 12 students, the instructor can see the quality of preparation, reasoning, and discussion behavior clearly.

Participation is graded at the individual level.

## Criteria

### 1. Preparation
Evidence that the student completed the reading, reviewed the client brief, and arrived ready to engage.

### 2. Analytical contribution
The student contributes ideas that use course frameworks rather than generic opinions.

### 3. Intellectual engagement
The student listens, builds on others' ideas, asks productive questions, and challenges assumptions constructively.

### 4. Communication quality
The student speaks clearly, structures points logically, and supports claims with evidence or framework logic.

### 5. Seminar citizenship
The student contributes to team effectiveness, stays on task, and helps the room think better.

## Performance levels

### Outstanding
Consistently advances the discussion with well-structured, framework-grounded, evidence-aware contributions. Builds the room's thinking.

### Strong
Prepared and engaged. Makes useful contributions regularly and participates constructively.

### Adequate
Shows some preparation and contributes at times, but analytical depth or consistency is limited.

### Minimal
Rare contribution, weak preparation signals, or passive presence.

## Practical note

In a 12-person seminar, absence of contribution is visible. Silence is not neutral.
