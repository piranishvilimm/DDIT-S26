# Syllabus Alignment Notes

This repository is built to align with the published course structure and the corrected Weekend 1 instructor guide.

## Weekend 1 scope

Weekend 1 covers:
- course orientation and seminar expectations,
- Rogers' five-domain model,
- Christensen's disruption framework,
- Uber, Airbnb, and Tesla as structured comparative cases,
- Yoo et al. as academic grounding for digital innovation,
- consulting methods including issue trees and hypothesis logs,
- competitive landscape methods,
- disruption diagnostic work,
- ROI as a conceptual primer only,
- inter-weekend sprint planning.

## Explicit exclusions for Weekend 1

The following do not belong in Weekend 1:
- STRIDE
- threat modeling
- data flow diagrams
- controls matrix work
- architecture decision records
- Responsible AI regulation detail
- EU AI Act detail
- OECD AI principles detail

Those belong in other contexts or later weekends.

## Cohort assumptions

- 12 students
- 3 teams of 4
- seminar-scale participation
- individual participation assessment with behavior anchors

## Dates

- Weekend 1: March 14-15, 2026
- Weekend 2: April 4-5, 2026
- Weekend 3: May 23-24, 2026
- Weekend 4: June 6-7, 2026

## Project continuity

Client industry context must be defined before Weekend 1. Teams carry that context across all four weekends.
